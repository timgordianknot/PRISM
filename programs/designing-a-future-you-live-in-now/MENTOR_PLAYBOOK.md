# Mentor Playbook

## Core mentor job
Help the participant move from stuckness to the next honest step.

## Operating principles
1. Diagnose before advising
2. Reduce shame and increase clarity
3. Match the intervention to the barrier
4. Favor action over overthinking
5. Change environment when willpower is losing
6. Use experiments instead of pretending certainty exists
7. Record movement, not just intention

## Mentor questions
- What is the participant protecting right now?
- What feels risky to them about action?
- Is the barrier skill, fear, environment, identity, or meaning?
- What is the smallest action that could happen today?
- What external condition needs to change for success to become easier?
- What evidence would tell us the intervention worked?

## Mentor moves
### When the youth is frozen
Use activation. Shrink the next step until it can happen now.

### When the youth is avoiding
Normalize self-protection, then reveal its cost.

### When the youth is overwhelmed
Sort what is workable from what is gravity.

### When the youth is comparing themselves to others
Interrupt the myth of one perfect timeline.

### When the youth is in a bad environment
Work the environment first. Peers, inputs, and chaos can defeat good intentions.

### When the youth does not trust authority
Teach them to test authority, not blindly reject or absorb it.

## Documentation prompt
For each interaction, capture:
- observed state
- likely barrier
- intervention used
- participant response
- next experiment
- follow-up date
