# Mentor Field Guide

## Use this when
A participant is frozen, avoidant, overwhelmed, cynical, ashamed, or caught in a loop.

## Step 1. Read the state
Ask:
- What is happening right now?
- What is the participant protecting?
- What is the cost of staying here?

## Step 2. Classify the barrier
### Skill gap
They do not know how.

### Self-protection
They are avoiding exposure, failure, or identity threat.

### Gravity problem
They are fighting an unchangeable condition.

### Environment mismatch
Their peers, inputs, space, or routines are sabotaging success.

### Identity conflict
Trying would make them visible in a new way.

### Meaning gap
The work does not feel connected to a reason that matters.

## Step 3. Match the move
### Frozen
Use the five-second move. Get the first physical action to happen fast.

### Beginner frustration
Use the twenty-hour ramp. Break the skill apart and normalize early incompetence.

### Overwhelm
Reduce the step size. Shrink the next action until it is real.

### Single-path panic
Use the three-path future. Create more than one possible way forward.

### Fear loop
Prototype before commitment. Test reality in a smaller form.

### Shame spiral
Convert comparison into evidence. Ask what data actually exists.

## Step 4. Check environment
Ask:
- Who are they conforming to?
- What inputs are shaping their mood and attention?
- What in their physical setup is adding friction?
- What could change today that would make success easier?

## Step 5. Document the interaction
Capture:
- observed state
- barrier category
- intervention used
- participant response
- next experiment
- follow-up timing

## Rule
Do not moralize what you have not yet diagnosed.

## Goal
Help the participant take the next honest step and gather evidence that change is possible.
