# Authority and Skepticism Filters

## Purpose
Teach mentors and participants to become more intentional about who and what they authorize to shape their worldview.

## Core distinctions
### Fact vs argument
A fact describes reality. An argument attempts to persuade.

### Expertise vs influence
Not all confident voices are competent. Not all competent voices deserve deep trust in every domain.

### Alignment vs authority
Even real expertise may not align with a participant's values, goals, or context.

## Mentor tasks
- help participants identify whose voice they are obeying
- test whether the source is credible in the relevant domain
- test whether the advice fits the participant's actual goal
- build curiosity without blind compliance

## Practical questions
- Who taught you this belief?
- Does that person have earned authority in this area?
- Does this belief create movement or paralysis?
- Is this a fact, an argument, or an inherited fear?
- What evidence would support or weaken this claim?

## Outcome
Participants learn not to absorb every message whole. They learn to examine, filter, and choose authority with intention.
