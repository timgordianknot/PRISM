# PRISM Integration

## Function in the larger system
This framework should serve as an integration layer across PRISM, especially anywhere youth or staff are being asked to move from theory into behavior.

## Integration roles
- **Diagnostic layer**: helps staff identify the actual barrier in front of the participant
- **Intervention layer**: helps staff choose the smallest right next move
- **Environment layer**: helps participants change surroundings, peers, and inputs
- **Meaning layer**: helps connect work view, life view, and identity
- **Measurement layer**: provides observable KPIs for improvement

## Suggested PRISM touchpoints
- Intake and early stabilization
- Mentor onboarding and staff training
- Cohort check-ins and case review
- Reflection, re-engagement, and transition planning
- Program replication and quality control

## Suggested workflow
1. Observe the participant state
2. Run stuckness diagnostics
3. Classify the issue
4. Choose an intervention heuristic
5. Adjust environment if needed
6. Capture what was tried
7. Review movement and update the next action

## Artifact relationship
This area should remain linked to the broader PRISM architecture while being modular enough to attach to future programs, staff handbooks, digital tools, and mentor training packs.
