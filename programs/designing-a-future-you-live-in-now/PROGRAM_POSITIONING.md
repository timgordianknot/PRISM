# Program Positioning

## Position inside PRISM
This can function in two valid ways inside PRISM:

### 1. Standalone program
A dedicated life-design and mentor-development program focused on helping participants identify barriers, test futures, build daily structure, and move from abstract hope into lived practice.

### 2. Attachment or integration layer
A module that attaches to other PRISM programs and converts insight into action. In this mode, it acts as the bridge between what participants learn and how they begin to live differently now.

## Recommended position
Treat this as a **core PRISM architecture area** with dual use:
- participant-facing program content
- staff-facing mentor training system

## Primary outcome
Participants and staff learn how to move from:
- confusion to diagnosis
- avoidance to activation
- fantasy to experimentation
- scattered effort to aligned systems
- delayed identity to practiced identity

## Who it is for
- Youth who feel stuck, behind, or trapped in self-protective patterns
- Staff and mentors who need better tools for intervention
- Programs that want a repeatable life-design framework instead of vague encouragement

## Distinctive value
This framework does not rely on generic motivation. It uses:
- diagnostic language
- action heuristics
- environmental design
- skepticism and authority filters
- measurable mentor behaviors and participant outcomes

## Strategic fit
Within PRISM, this area helps standardize mentor quality and intervention timing. That makes it useful not only for youth development, but also for staff training, cohort stabilization, and long-range program replication.
