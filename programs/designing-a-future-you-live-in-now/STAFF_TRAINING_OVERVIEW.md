# Staff Training Overview

## Training goal
Train staff and mentors to recognize why a youth is stuck, avoid moralizing or overreacting, and deploy a useful intervention that increases movement without increasing shame.

## Staff competencies
Staff should be able to:
- tell the difference between a skill deficit and a self-protection strategy
- identify when a problem is workable versus when it must be accepted as a circumstance
- notice when environment is undermining effort
- use small activation strategies instead of waiting for motivation
- guide youth into experiments instead of fake certainty
- measure progress using observable evidence

## Training sequence
### 1. Diagnostic thinking
Teach staff to ask: what is actually happening here?

### 2. Barrier classification
Is this:
- a knowledge gap
- a fear response
- an environment problem
- an identity problem
- a meaning problem
- a systems problem

### 3. Intervention matching
Choose the smallest intervention that has the best chance of producing forward motion.

### 4. Reflection and review
Log what was tried, what happened, and what should happen next.

## Staff standard
The mentor is not there to rescue, dominate, or perform expertise. The mentor is there to improve clarity, timing, environment, and next-step quality.

## Recommended outputs
- mentor training deck
- one-page field guide
- case review protocol
- intervention tracking form
- observable KPI sheet
