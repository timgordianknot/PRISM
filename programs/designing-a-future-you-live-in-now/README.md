# Designing a Future You Live In Now

## Purpose
This area gives PRISM a permanent home for a mentor-facing and participant-facing framework that helps people turn life design into present-day practice.

The core premise is simple: youth do not move forward through inspiration alone. They need diagnostic clarity, the right intervention at the right time, environmental support, and measurable movement.

## Why this belongs in PRISM
This work is not a side note. It is core architecture for how PRISM trains mentors, staff, and facilitators to recognize stuckness, respond well, and help participants build futures they can begin living now.

## Core design statement
**Designing a Future You Live In Now** is a PRISM framework for translating stuckness diagnostics into present-day action, environmental alignment, and measurable growth.

## What lives here
- Program positioning and fit within PRISM
- Staff training overview
- Mentor playbook
- Stuckness diagnostics
- Intervention heuristics
- Environmental firewall guidance
- Authority and skepticism filters
- KPIs and evaluation model
- Session outlines, tools, and assessments

## Working intent
This framework is being developed to help train staff better, strengthen mentor placement and intervention quality, and improve the odds that one more well-positioned mentor can help change one more life.

## Initial module spine
1. Name the future
2. Diagnose the stuck point
3. Distinguish skill gap from self-protection
4. Apply the smallest useful intervention
5. Reshape environment and inputs
6. Prototype the next self in real life
7. Measure movement and iterate

## Current development status
Ongoing. This folder is the active build area for the program/module and its staff-training architecture.
