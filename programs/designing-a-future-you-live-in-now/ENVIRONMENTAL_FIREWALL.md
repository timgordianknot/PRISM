# Environmental Firewall

## Principle
Environment often beats intention. If the surroundings constantly cue distraction, fear, comparison, or chaos, motivation alone will not hold.

## Mentor tasks
- evaluate peer influence
- evaluate media and input load
- evaluate physical organization and friction
- identify cue patterns that support or sabotage action
- help the participant build a better daily default

## Environmental levers
### Peer group
People tend to conform to the standards around them.

### Information diet
What goes in repeatedly shapes emotional state, expectations, and attention.

### Physical space
A disordered or inaccessible space can silently raise the cost of every good action.

### Routines and cues
Consistent triggers reduce dependence on mood.

## Mentor prompts
- Who are you becoming more like by staying in this environment?
- What input is feeding fear, anger, or hopelessness?
- What part of the room, routine, or tool access can we change today?
- What would make the desired action easier by default?

## Rule of thumb
When effort keeps collapsing, inspect the environment before blaming the person.
