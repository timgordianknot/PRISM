# Stuckness Diagnostics

## Purpose
This document helps mentors classify what kind of stuckness is present before deciding what to do.

## Diagnostic categories
### 1. Skill gap
The participant does not yet know how.

**Signal:** confusion, poor technique, repeated errors with no model.

### 2. Self-protection pattern
The participant is protecting worth, identity, or status by delaying action.

**Signal:** procrastination, excuse-building, perfectionism, endless planning, refusal to test ability.

### 3. Gravity problem
The participant is trying to solve an unchangeable condition as if it were a normal task.

**Signal:** chronic frustration aimed at fixed circumstances.

### 4. Environment mismatch
The participant's surroundings are defeating their stated goals.

**Signal:** peer drag, chaos, negative inputs, no structure, no access, no cues.

### 5. Identity conflict
The participant is attached to an old self-concept or afraid of becoming visible in a new one.

**Signal:** sabotage after progress, embarrassment around trying, retreat into familiar failure.

### 6. Meaning gap
The participant cannot connect effort to a reason that matters.

**Signal:** flat engagement, low ownership, cynical detachment.

## Quick decision path
1. Can they do the task with support?
2. If not, is it missing skill or fear of exposure?
3. Is the problem actually workable?
4. Is the environment making success unnecessarily hard?
5. Is there a self-story blocking movement?
6. Is the reason for effort alive enough to matter?

## Mentor caution
Do not confuse resistance with laziness. Resistance often contains information.
